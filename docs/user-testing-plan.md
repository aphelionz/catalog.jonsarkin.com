# User Testing Plan

## Goal
Find where people get stuck navigating the catalog and acquiring works across catalog.jonsarkin.com and jonsarkin.com.

## Participants
3-5 people. Mix of:
- Someone who collects art (knows gallery conventions)
- Someone who doesn't (represents casual visitors)
- Someone who's never seen either site

## Setup
Screen share (Zoom/FaceTime), ask them to think aloud. Watch silently, take notes. ~15 minutes per person.

## Tasks

### 1. Open-ended browse
> "You've heard about Jon Sarkin's work. Go to jonsarkin.com and explore — tell me what you see and what interests you."

Watching for: Do they find the catalog? Do they understand the two-site relationship? Does the navigation make sense?

### 2. Find a specific work
> "Find a piece made with oil pastel from 2017."

Watching for: Do they use faceted browse, search, or just scroll? Do they go to the catalog or stay on Shopify? Do facet options make sense?

### 3. Acquire a work
> "You've found something you like. How would you go about getting it?"

Watching for: Do they find the [acquire] button? Is the catalog-to-Shopify handoff confusing? Does "Acquire This Work" make sense vs. "Add to Cart"?

### 4. High-value inquiry
> "Now look at [specific expensive piece]. How would you ask about purchasing this one?"

Watching for: Do they understand the inquire flow? Does the contact form pre-fill correctly?

### 5. Return visit
> "You want to come back and find that first piece again. How would you do that?"

Watching for: Do they bookmark? Search? Remember the path?

## What to record for each task
- Did they complete it? How long?
- Where did they hesitate or backtrack?
- What did they say out loud?
- Any moments of confusion or surprise?

## Rules
- Don't help them or explain anything — just say "what would you try?"
- Don't ask leading questions — "was the navigation confusing?" is bad, "tell me what you're thinking" is good
- Let silences sit — they'll fill them with useful observations
